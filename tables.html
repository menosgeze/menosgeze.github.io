My research was to compute the Tate Shafarevich group order and type of as many rational elliptic curves as possible assuming standard conjectures. Here, I present you the results of my computations. I also present some results on the p-adic height of rank 1 rational elliptic curves.

Table 1: Rational elliptic curves

I consider all elliptic curves in Cremona's database and these up to conductor one million in Stein Watkins' database. For rank 0 curves, these databases already contain the Tate Shafarevich group order. Using descent methods in Magma and Tom Fisher's results from 5 descent, I found the Tate-Shafarevich group invariants listed here, under the assumption of the Birch and Swinnerton Dyer conjecture and the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis (for computing ideal class groups.) For positive rank curves, I performed point searches in SageMath using the Cocalc server. And in some cases, I also assumed the p-adic BSD conjecture as stated by Mazur, Tate and Teiltelbaum for rank 1 elliptic curves, together with Wuthrich's numerical modular symbols.

For most rank 1 rational elliptic curves, I found a generator of their Mordell-Weil group. In the file Rank1-gen-unknown, I list the elliptic curves where I did not find such generator. Table 3: Rational elliptic curves of rank larger than 1

I considered all elliptic curves in Cremona's database and these up to conductor one million in Stein Watkins' database of conductors 2, 3 and 4. These databases do not contain the Tate Shafarevich group order of these curves. Using point search methods in SageMath (which uses Michael Stoll's ratpoints module in PARI/GP) and point search methods in curves obtained by Magma's descent methods, I found the generators of most curves. Using a Diophantine inequality, I was able to determine the Ш group order of several rank 2 elliptic curves where I did not find the a second generator of the Mordell Weil group.
